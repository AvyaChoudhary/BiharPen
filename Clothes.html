The Clothes Of Bihar

Traditional attires of Bihar seamlessly blend the essence of culture, comfort, and style. Bihar dresses the way it should: the culture is not only depicted but also felt. Every piece has a story behind it, from handwoven silk sarees to the practical gamchha. This style is timeless yet rooted in tradition, making Bihari fashion something special to learn and hold onto.

For women, sarees are an ultimate style statement, and Bihar has its own treasures as well. The famous Tussar silk saree, soft and earthy tone, has a special appeal for special occasions. Bhagalpuri silk sarees are truly elegant and beautiful to behold. Sarees are nothing but a part of the state's cultural heritage, which is woven with tradition and care. Younger girls prefer the lehenga-choli in festivities or weddings, adding a dash of glam due to striking colors and intricate embroidery.

Dhoti - be it a kurta or simple dhoti - is the age-old classic attire for men. It is a long piece of cloth, wrapped skillfully around the waist, which bestows an impression of a traditional stand-up, polo-necked kurta. Be it a wedding, festival, or just another day, the dhoti never goes out of fashion. Then there's lungi, chill cousin of dhoti. They're really easy to wear, super comfy, and perfect for casual days when the vibe is all about taking it easy.

No Bihari outfit is complete without the iconic gamchha. This simple cotton cloth is the ultimate multitasker, used as a scarf, towel, or even to carry things. It’s colorful, lightweight, and something you’ll spot everywhere in Bihar. Accessories also play a big role in Bihari fashion. Women love their jhumkas (dangly earrings), bangles, and payal (anklets), while men often add turbans or pagdis during special occasions for that extra touch of tradition.
